    From the dizzying altitudes of Nepal we descend south into India which, with 1.4 billion people, has overtaken China as the world's most populous country. 
    
    India is a land rich in vibrancy and diversity. From snow-capped Himalayas to rainforests to deserts to beach resorts. From immense modern cities to sprawling makeshift slums. It gave the world chess, the number zero, cotton textiles, and spices that literally changed the course of history. As if all that wasn't enough, India is the birthplace of four major world religions: Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism.
    
    Our first game, Go Goa, puts players in a frantic race around the popular region of Goa. Having chosen their itineraries, players race to complete their chosen itineraries and 'tick off' a range of historic, geographic, religious and touristic sites. Best laid plans can easily be thwarted by the roll of the dice, but there are ways to boost your score and manage your luck.
    
    Next up we travel to the bustling market of Jaipur - a classic 2-player card battle in which players jostle to grab the prized goods from gold and diamonds, to leather and spices. Timing and hand management are critical to sell your goods to your best advantage while thwarting your opponent.
