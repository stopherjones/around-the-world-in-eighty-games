    From Iraq, We travel north east again, past Iran and the Caspian Sea to Uzbekistan, one of the world's few doubly landlocked countries — meaning you have to cross two borders just to reach the sea.
    Uzbekistan sits on the ancient Silk Road, the legendary trade network that connected China to Europe for centuries, and its cities — Samarkand, Bukhara, Tashkent — were once among the most important and dazzling in the world. Think towering minarets, turquoise domes, and bustling bazaars where silk, spices and precious stones changed hands between merchants from dozens of civilizations.
    It is in one of these ancient cities, Khiva, where we play our next game, taking on the role of such merchants, navigating the Silk Road during its golden age.
    In Khiva, players play and pass cards in this tug of war game as they jostle to bring the best caravans to their markets and compete for the richest goods.
