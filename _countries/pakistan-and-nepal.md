intro: |

    From Uzbekistan we travel south-east towards India, but we’re not taking the easy route, we’re donning our crampons and oxygen tanks for a spot of serious mountaineering!

    We head off in a straight line through Tajikistan, following the old Silk Road through the narrow Wakhan Corridor, before heading into the Karakoram Mountain Range along the China-Pakistan border. From here we approach the legendary K2, the world’s second highest mountain at 8,611m. We’ll make the ascent the hard way, up the West Ridge, before dropping down the more common Abruzzi Spur to the southeast.

    After catching our breath, we’ll head southwest following the Indus River through Pakistani Kashmir to Nanga Parbat, the world’s 9th highest mountain at ‘only’ 8,126m. But don’t be fooled, because Nanga Parbat has earned the nickname ‘Killer Mountain’ due to its challenging ascent and high fatality rate among climbers! We’ll ascend from the northeast up the Rakhiot Face, before descending southeast down the Rupal Face towards Nepal and the last leg on our mountaineering adventure.

    Finally, we head further southeast through Nepal on our way to India. Unfortunately, we don’t get to climb Everest itself while we’re in Nepal, as we split up, exploring the mountain villages of Dunai and Kagkot, take a spiritual retreat at the Jampa Monastery, or opt for more climbing at Dhaulagiri, the 7th highest mountain in the world at 8,167m.

    To achieve these feats, we have three very different climbing-themed games – K2, Nanga Parbat and Trek12.

    K2, like the mountain for which it is named, is an unforgiving game requiring careful route-planning, card management, and a little jostling for position on the mountain itself. As you ascend the mountain, pitch your camps at strategic locations, keeping an eye on the weather before making your push to the summit. Survival in this game is far from guaranteed!

    Nanga Parbat gives us a glimpse into mountaineering from the Sherpas’ perspective, as you compete to trap animals, build camps and guide your hikers up the mountain ahead of your competition! It’s a tight game, quite abstract in its strategy and manoeuvring to secure the optimum placement on the board.

    Finally Trek12 is a simple Roll-n-Write with surprising depth and no shortage of luck needed to score well. Points are scored for creating chains of consecutive numbers and areas of matching numbers. We’ll play from a random selection of the standard maps, all based around Nepal, but watch out for the placement rules unique to each map.
