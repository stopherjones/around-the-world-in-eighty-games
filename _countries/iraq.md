    We travel north east across Saudi Arabia and into Iraq, the home of ancient Mesopotamian sites like Babylon and Ur, where writing, law, and urban life first took shape. 

    While Iraq has faced decades of conflict that still shape external perceptions, Iraqi culture is defined by warmth and hospitality, with strong traditions around food, poetry, and storytelling. 
    
    The landscape shifts dramatically from rugged mountains, to desert plains, to the palm-lined banks of the Tigris and Euphrates rivers. And it is in these fertile plains where we play our next two games, Assyria, and Tigris & Euphrates. 

    In Assyria, players expand from their initial Ziggurat, pitching tents and gathering resources to place and develop Ziggurats, and make offerings to the Gods. But famine is never far away and players will often have to sacrifice both tents and turn order to try and achieve their longer-term strategies.  
    
    Regarded by many as Reiner Knizia's masterpiece, Tigris & Euphrates is a classic tile placement / area majority game where players compete to build balanced civilisations across farming, trading, religion, and government. Conflict occurs when  civilizations connect with only one leader of each type surviving such a conflict.
